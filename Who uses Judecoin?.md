# Who uses Judecoin?

Judecoin is used by people who value privacy, decentralization, censorship resistance, and open digital money.

JUDE can be used by individuals, wallet users, Service Node operators, developers, researchers, merchants, businesses, infrastructure operators, documentation contributors, and community members.

## Individuals

Individuals use Judecoin as private electronic cash.

Judecoin is designed for people who want to send, receive, and store value without exposing their full transaction history to the public.

Individual users may include:

- users who value financial privacy
- users who need censorship-resistant payments
- users who want to control their own funds
- users who prefer decentralized digital money
- users who want to reduce reliance on banks and payment intermediaries
- users who need direct cross-border value transfer

Judecoin gives individuals control, privacy, and freedom in digital transactions.

## Wallet users

Wallet users interact with Judecoin through desktop, mobile, or command-line wallets.

Wallets allow users to:

- receive JUDE
- send JUDE
- check balances
- manage addresses
- back up and recover accounts
- connect to the Judecoin network

Wallet users are the foundation of real network activity.

## Service Node operators

Service Node operators help support Judecoin’s decentralized infrastructure.

With Proof of Stake, Service Node operators participate in network operation, stability, and long-term ecosystem development.

Service Node operators may:

- run stable nodes
- maintain uptime
- monitor node status
- follow upgrade instructions
- help secure the network
- support decentralized infrastructure
- participate in Judecoin’s long-term development

A strong Service Node operator base strengthens the network.

## Developers

Developers build and improve the Judecoin ecosystem.

Developer contributions may include:

- wallet improvements
- daemon software
- Service Node tools
- RPC integrations
- monitoring dashboards
- atomic swap tools
- cross-chain tools
- documentation updates
- testing and debugging

Developers are essential to Judecoin’s long-term growth and reliability.

## Researchers

Judecoin is connected to research in privacy, cryptography, blockchain security, distributed systems, and cross-chain technology.

Researchers may study or contribute to:

- transaction privacy
- ring signatures
- zero-knowledge proof compatibility
- censorship resistance
- wallet security
- network-level privacy
- blockchain scalability
- atomic swap design
- protocol security

Research helps Judecoin continue improving as a privacy-focused cryptocurrency.

## Merchants and payment users

Merchants and payment users can use Judecoin for private, low-cost, and direct digital payments.

Potential use cases include:

- direct payments
- online payments
- cross-border payments
- merchant payments
- private settlement
- digital commerce

Judecoin gives merchants an alternative form of digital cash without automatic chargebacks and with reduced reliance on traditional payment intermediaries.

## Businesses

Businesses can use Judecoin to reach users who prefer digital asset payments and private financial tools.

Businesses may benefit from:

- low-cost payment options
- no automatic chargebacks
- new payment users
- community visibility
- cross-border settlement
- direct digital transactions

Accepting Judecoin can connect businesses with a global digital currency community.

## Infrastructure operators

Infrastructure operators help maintain the tools and systems around the Judecoin network.

They may work on:

- nodes
- block explorers
- monitoring tools
- network status pages
- Service Node dashboards
- public statistics resources
- deployment automation
- network health tools

Infrastructure operators make Judecoin easier to observe, maintain, and use.

## Documentation contributors

Documentation contributors help users, developers, and operators understand how Judecoin works.

They may contribute by:

- improving guides
- updating outdated pages
- organizing documentation
- translating materials
- correcting technical instructions
- writing clearer explanations
- maintaining public knowledge resources

Good documentation helps Judecoin grow faster and reduces confusion for new users.

## Community contributors

Community contributors help Judecoin grow through education, translation, testing, communication, and ecosystem organization.

Community contributions may include:

- writing guides
- translating materials
- answering user questions
- reporting bugs
- organizing information
- improving public documentation
- supporting new users
- sharing official updates
- building community resources

A strong community keeps Judecoin open, active, and resilient.

## New users

New users may begin by learning what Judecoin is, installing a wallet, understanding wallet backup, and following official updates.

New users should learn how to protect wallet information, verify official software, and understand how private digital currency works before sending funds.

Every new user strengthens the Judecoin network.

## Summary

Judecoin is used and supported by people who value privacy, decentralization, censorship resistance, and open financial infrastructure.

Its ecosystem includes individual users, wallet users, Service Node operators, developers, researchers, merchants, businesses, infrastructure operators, documentation contributors, and community contributors.

JUDE is for users who want private digital cash and for builders who want to create the next generation of decentralized financial infrastructure.
