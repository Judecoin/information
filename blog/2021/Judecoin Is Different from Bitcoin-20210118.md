# Judecoin Is Different from Bitcoin

> **Date:** January 18, 2021  
> **Category:** News

"Judecoin is a secure, private, and hard-to-track currency," its website says. Some might think the same definition applies to Bitcoin, but this is not true when it comes to privacy and untraceability.

Judecoin is a digital currency that uses different types of cryptography to achieve these characteristics. This article aims to illustrate the differences between Judecoin and Bitcoin.

## Contrast

There is a common misconception that Bitcoin is a completely untraceable digital currency.

The premise of Bitcoin is that all transactions can be viewed on the blockchain. This allows anyone to verify transactions, especially for use cases that require full transparency, such as government and non-profit spending.

Judecoin differs from Bitcoin in that it focuses on the privacy of digital currency transactions. Judecoin uses different types of technology to make transactions untraceable and hide balances.

Individual Bitcoin addresses are publicly shared, allowing anyone to view your balance and all other Bitcoin addresses you have ever traded with. Although the blockchain does not specify a name, if someone decides to share their address publicly, all activity associated with that address will forever be associated with that person.

People can know that you have an address even if you do not make that information public. Simply making a transaction with another tagged address is enough for others to determine that an account belongs to you.

For example, balances and transactions associated with WikiLeaks' public donation addresses can be found through public blockchain records.

While some organizations with a need for transparency may want to display their trading histories, not all individuals and companies do.

Imagine that anyone could see all your bank balances and transactions. You probably would not want everyone else to know about your salary and spending habits.

Financial privacy is especially important in the wake of a series of security breaches over the past few years that exposed credit card data. You do not necessarily want this information stored in every store and website you buy from.

Judecoin protects the value of this personal information and keeps transaction details private by default.

## Technology

Without delving into the technical details, Judecoin makes transactions untraceable through ring signature technology.

It mixes individual transactions with other transactions, so it is not clear to the public who owns which address. It also hides balances through hidden addresses, which are random, one-time addresses that cannot be associated with an individual.

Judecoin also has a "spend key" that is used to spend money, and a "view key" that can be shared with others.

The view key allows selected people to transparently view transaction details. Optional transparency is beneficial in cases where an entity needs to be audited or where information is submitted for tax purposes.

Separating this into two different keys ensures that third parties can only see the details of transactions, but cannot spend the funds.

Using an analogy with the existing financial system: the bank login information required to access your money is like a spend key, while a copy of a bank account statement and transaction details is like a view key.
