# JUDE's Proof of Stake (PoS) Plan

> **Date:** June 17, 2024  
> **Category:** Notice

JUDE's Proof of Stake (PoS) plan has many reasons for implementation, including supporting more popular blockchain consensus mechanisms and making the JUDE network more secure, sustainable, and powerful.

JUDE service nodes are supported by reward nodes for network functions, scalability, and decentralized technology stacks.

In order to operate a service node, the operator can lock at least `23,600 JUDE`.

The staking transaction will be submitted to the network. After a short delay, the service node will be registered and begin contributing to the network.

In return for their services, service node operators receive a portion of the average block reward from each block mined on the JUDE blockchain.

JUDE shared nodes require a creator and multiple contributors, with no more than nine contributors.

For the staking requirements, shared nodes need to stake at least `23,600 JUDE` and then start providing the network with a minimum level of bandwidth and storage.

The JUDE network is run by a decentralized network of incentivized nodes called service nodes.

As the main force of the JUDE network, service nodes receive a share of rewards, up to `8.96 JUDE` per block.

The initial staking is on-chain, and the unlocking period is `180 days`.

In the future, the community plans to develop a `30-day` unlocking period and a `360-day` unlocking period.

Detailed documentation for the setup guides of service nodes and shared nodes will be updated simultaneously on the official website and GitHub in the future.
