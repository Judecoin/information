## About Judecoin

### Q1. What is Judecoin?

Judecoin is a leading cryptocurrency focused on private and censorship-resistant transactions.

Most existing cryptocurrencies, including Bitcoin and Ethereum, use transparent blockchains. This means transactions can be publicly verified and traced, and the sending and receiving addresses may be linked to a person’s real-world identity.

Judecoin uses privacy-oriented cryptographic technology to protect sending addresses, receiving addresses, and transaction details.

Judecoin also adopts atomic swap technology to realize online and offline cross-chain transactions while ensuring data security and privacy.

### Q2. What is JUDE?

JUDE is the native currency of the Judecoin network.

JUDE is used for private transactions, value transfer, network participation, Service Node operation, atomic swaps, and future ecosystem applications.

JUDE is the core unit of value within the Judecoin ecosystem.

### Q3. How is Judecoin different from other digital currencies?

Judecoin is different from many digital currencies in several important ways:

1. Judecoin is developed based on the underlying technology of blockchain. Its decentralized distributed ledger provides a foundation for secure and scalable digital transactions.

2. Judecoin is based on the CryptoNote protocol. Its privacy-oriented design obscures blockchain transactions, focuses on private and censorship-resistant transactions, and helps ensure that transactions are confidential and untraceable.

3. Judecoin is an open-source community project. It uses the RandomJDX algorithm and attracts cryptocurrency researchers, developers, and engineering talents from around the world.

4. Judecoin adopts blockchain atomic swap technology to realize cross-chain transactions with other blockchains and to achieve broader communication between blockchain networks, forming a foundation for wider interconnection.

5. Judecoin is evolving into a broader ecosystem with wallets, Service Nodes, Proof of Stake participation, privacy research, cross-chain tools, merchant adoption, and long-term decentralized infrastructure.

## Privacy and fungibility

### Q4. Is Judecoin private by default?

Yes.

Judecoin is designed around default privacy. It protects transaction information by default, helping prevent transaction history from being easily linked to real-world identities or used for censorship.

Judecoin’s privacy design helps users transact without exposing unnecessary financial information to the public.

### Q5. Why does privacy matter?

Privacy matters because transparent transaction histories can expose personal, commercial, and financial information.

Financial privacy protects users from surveillance, profiling, discrimination, and censorship based on transaction history.

Privacy is not only a technical feature. It is an essential part of financial freedom.

### Q6. What does fungibility mean?

Fungibility means that each unit of a currency should be treated equally.

For digital cash, fungibility is essential. Coins should not be rejected, censored, or treated differently because of their previous transaction history.

Judecoin’s privacy-oriented design helps protect fungibility by reducing the ability to trace and label coins based on past transactions.

## Service Nodes and Proof of Stake

### Q17. What are Service Nodes?

Service Nodes are part of Judecoin’s decentralized network infrastructure.

With Proof of Stake, Service Nodes help support network operation, stability, reward distribution, and long-term ecosystem development.

Service Nodes give JUDE holders a way to participate directly in the operation of the network.

### Q18. What is Proof of Stake?

Proof of Stake is a network participation model in which eligible participants support the network by staking coins and operating infrastructure.

In Judecoin, Proof of Stake is connected to Service Node operation, network stability, and long-term participation by JUDE holders.

Proof of Stake turns JUDE from only a transaction currency into a currency connected to network participation.

### Q19. Why are Service Nodes important?

Service Nodes are important because they strengthen decentralized infrastructure.

A strong Service Node network supports stability, reliability, network participation, and the long-term development of Judecoin.

Service Nodes help Judecoin move from a currency network into a broader decentralized infrastructure network.

## Atomic swaps and ecosystem

### Q21. What are atomic swaps?

Atomic swaps are a cross-chain exchange method that allows assets from different blockchains to be exchanged without relying entirely on centralized intermediaries.

Judecoin adopts atomic swap technology to support cross-chain transactions and communication between blockchain networks.

Atomic swaps help Judecoin connect with other blockchain ecosystems while preserving the spirit of decentralization.

## Wallets and safety

### Q23. Where can I get a Judecoin wallet?

Users should also be careful with unofficial downloads, phishing links, fake support accounts, and unknown third-party tools.
